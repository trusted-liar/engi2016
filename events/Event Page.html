Date: 13th Feb, 2016

Time: 12pm – 3pm

Venue: B.R. Ambedkar Auditorium

The term “Folk dance” describes all forms of folk and tribal dances in regions across India. While Indian classical dance was considered a higher form of art and was practiced in courts, temples and on special occasions, folk dance forms were and are still practiced in groups in rural areas as an expression of the daily work and rituals of the common people during festivals. The folk dance competition truly depicts the varied culture and traditions of India. With performances ranging from Gujarat's Garba to Assam's Bihu dance, and from Punjab's Bhangra to Goa's Ghode Modni, the folk dance competition, Anushthaan, has been and will continue to add colours to the Engifest.
