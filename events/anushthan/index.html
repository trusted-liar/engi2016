Gujarat's Garba to Assam's Bihu dance, and from Punjab's Bhangra to Goa's Ghode Modni gives us a feeling of the rich culture that we all associate ourselves with. ANUSHTHAAN adds to the glory of the culture by assembling the best folk-dancers on a common stage.The audience eagerly awaits the rich expressions and vibrancy the dancers present.
Date: 13 Feb
Time: 12:00 pm
Venue: BR Ambedkar Auditorium
Fees: 200 / team
1. Teams have to perform one of the following dance forms:
Alkap, Bhangra, Bhavai, Bihu, Cheraw (bamboo dance) , Chhau, Dhap, Dumhal, Gambhira,Garba (dandiya), Gaur dance, Ghoomar, Ghumura, Giddha, Gondhal, Jawara ,Jhoomer, Kalbelia, Karakattam, Koli, Lavani, Naga dance, Odissi, Padhar, Raas.
If team wants to perform a form other than those mentioned above, they are required to contact the coordinators well in advance.
2. The teams must bring CDs/ Pen-drive for the music (audio format).
3. The total time of performance should range from 7 to 10 minutes.(One minute will be allowed in addition to maximum time for setup and strike of props)
4. The team size should be between 6 and 15.
5. The performance will be judged on the basis of Choreography, Synchronization, Expression, Creativity, Prop Usage, Costume, Energy, Stage coverage and overall impact.
6. All props are allowed except for the following:
Swords, knives or any other sharp object (unless plastic - but must be approved prior to performance)
Fire in any form (candles, torches, etc)
Fog or smoke
Helium balloons
Special lighting
7. In case of conflicts, decision of the judge is considered final and binding.
